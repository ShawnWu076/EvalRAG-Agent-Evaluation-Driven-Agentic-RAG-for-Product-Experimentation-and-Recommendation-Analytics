# Segment Analysis

## Purpose

Segment analysis checks whether the treatment effect is consistent across important user, market, device, and product groups. It helps detect hidden harm and identifies where a partial rollout may be safe.

## Recommended Segments

Common segments include new users, returning users, high-value users, low-activity users, country, city, device, app version, traffic source, category, seller or creator tier, and risk or trust cohorts. Choose segments from product risk before reading every possible slice.

## Overall Win With Segment Harm

An overall win can hide damage to a strategic segment. For example, revenue may rise overall because new users click more, while high-value users retain less or complain more. Do not fully launch when a strategically important segment is harmed unless the tradeoff is explicitly accepted.

## Multiple Testing

Segment analysis can create false positives if teams inspect many slices without discipline. Use pre-specified segments when possible, look for practical magnitude and consistency, and treat exploratory findings as hypotheses for follow-up.

## Partial Rollout

Partial rollout is appropriate when the treatment is beneficial in some segments and harmful or uncertain in others. The rollout plan should define inclusion rules, monitoring metrics, and rollback criteria.
