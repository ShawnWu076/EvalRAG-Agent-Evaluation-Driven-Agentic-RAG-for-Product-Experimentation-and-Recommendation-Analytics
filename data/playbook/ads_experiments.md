# Ads Experiments

## Ads-Specific Tradeoffs

Ads experiments must balance platform revenue, advertiser value, and user experience. A change that increases ad clicks can still be harmful if advertiser conversion quality falls, if user complaints rise, or if long-term retention drops.

## Metrics

Common metrics include CTR, CVR, CPA, ROAS, revenue per mille, ad load, advertiser spend, conversion quality, complaint rate, and retention. CTR is an intermediate metric. It should not override downstream outcomes such as conversion value or advertiser ROI.

## Launch Guidance

Launch when revenue or advertiser value improves without meaningful harm to user guardrails or advertiser quality. Investigate further when CTR improves but CVR, ROAS, or retention worsens. A partial rollout can be appropriate when benefits are limited to specific advertiser categories or markets.

## Marketplace and Auction Effects

Ads systems can shift auction dynamics. Check whether treatment changes traffic quality, bid landscape, budget pacing, or the mix of advertisers winning impressions. Segment by advertiser size, campaign objective, placement, and market before broad launch.
