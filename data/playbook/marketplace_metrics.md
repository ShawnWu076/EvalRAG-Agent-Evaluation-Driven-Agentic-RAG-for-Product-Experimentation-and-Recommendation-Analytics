# Marketplace Metrics

## Marketplace Health

Marketplace experiments often affect multiple sides of the market. A buyer-facing improvement can harm sellers, couriers, creators, advertisers, or supply quality. Launch decisions should consider whether the treatment improves total marketplace value rather than moving value from one side to another.

## Common Metrics

Buyer metrics include conversion rate, retention, revenue per user, refund rate, and complaint rate. Seller or supply metrics include impressions, order volume, fulfillment quality, cancellation rate, payout, inventory depth, and churn. Platform metrics include gross merchandise value, margin, latency, and trust metrics.

## Guardrails

Guardrails should protect marketplace liquidity, fairness, quality, and trust. If a ranking model increases short-term revenue by concentrating exposure on a few sellers or creators, inspect diversity and long-term supply health before launch.

## Segment and Market Checks

Segment by country, city, category, supply type, buyer lifecycle stage, and seller size. Marketplace experiments can look positive overall while harming a strategically important group. Hidden harm should trigger investigation or restricted rollout.
